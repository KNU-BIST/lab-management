01 · Research

What we investigate

Our lab leverages a unique access to clinical-grade patient samples — CSF, blood, and brain tissue — to study neurological disease at the molecular level, alongside cancer biology and placental physiology.

Neurology

iNPH Biomarker Discovery

Using patient-derived CSF and blood samples to identify molecular markers of idiopathic normal pressure hydrocephalus, shunt responsiveness, and differential diagnosis from other neurodegenerative diseases.

Transcriptomics

Brain Tissue RNA-seq

Collecting and profiling surgically resected brain tissue — including glioblastoma, brain metastases, and iNPH — by RNA sequencing to decode disease-specific gene expression landscapes.

Immunophenotyping

Flow Cytometry Panels

Establishing and optimising FACS panels for immune profiling of iNPH patient blood and brain tissue to characterise immune cell populations in neurological disease.

Cancer Biology

Colorectal Cancer & Radioresistance

Investigating the molecular mechanisms underlying resistance to radiotherapy in colorectal cancer, with experience in cloning, cancer therapeutics development, and molecular biology.

Placental Biology

Gestational Diabetes & RAS

Characterising the renin-angiotensin system, ACE2, and glucose transporter expression in placental tissue from gestational diabetes cohorts and the modulatory role of aspirin.

Evidence Synthesis

Systematic Reviews & Meta-analyses

Conducting rigorous systematic reviews and meta-analyses in hydrocephalus surgery and iNPH differential diagnosis to synthesise the global evidence base and inform clinical practice.

05 · Biobank

Clinical sample access

A key strength of our lab is privileged access to rare, well-annotated patient-derived clinical samples through our PI's neurosurgical practice.

CSF
Cerebrospinal fluid from iNPH patients. Used for biomarker discovery, microplastic detection, and shunt responsiveness studies.
–80°C · Ongoing collection
Blood / PBMC
Peripheral blood from iNPH patients. Used for immune profiling by FACS and serum biomarker ELISA studies.
–80°C · Ongoing collection
Brain Tissue
Surgically resected tissue from GBM, brain metastases, and iNPH cases. Stored in TRIzol for RNA-seq; shared with Prof. Kim for electrophysiology.
–80°C in TRIzol · IRB approved
Placenta & Serum
Placental tissue and paired serum from gestational diabetes cohorts, in collaboration with Dr. Kim (Dept. OB/GYN).
–80°C · IRB approved

All sample collection and use is conducted under approved IRB protocols. Enquiries regarding sample collaboration: see contact below.
